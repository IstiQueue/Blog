Red Album Review

With 10 extended tracks, the album Red (Taylor’s Version) wrapped up a total 2 hours and 10 minutes long, a roller-coaster of mixed emotions.

In 2012, I remember jumping around in my room, screaming to the lyrics of ‘We Are Never Ever Getting Back Together’ and thinking, there is no way I would get this ecstatic feeling of listening to feel good songs and if I had to, I would pay to get it back. In 2012, I remember how much adoration I have for the magnificent woman who came up with the utmost heart wrenching lyrics a ten year old me has ever heard. In 2012, living with busy parents, no one had ever taught me about letting a feeling out, until I stumbled upon Taylor Swift’s album ‘Red’. Like the color, it symbolizes bravery, love, and heartbreak. At that time, it meant the entire world for me and I can proudly announce that it was one of the most impactful survival kits to grow up with.

In 2021, never in my wildest dreams I would have gotten to re-experience the mentioned ecstatic feeling back, and even better (for free too! if the Spotify subscription doesn’t count). In 2021, the adoration remains the same, it tripled actually, and yet, as a nineteen year old youth growing, my heart still gets wrenched, especially with the extended version of ‘All Too Well’. In 2021, I have learned so many things about the world. My parents remain busy though, but I got to cross paths with the one that introduced me to the constant feeling, Taylor Swift’s album ‘Red (Taylor’s Version)’. At this time, it still means the world to me, moreover it instantly became the footnotes in the story of my life.

With 10 extended tracks, the album Red (Taylor’s Version) wrapped up a total 2 hours and 10 minutes long, a roller-coaster of mixed emotions. Including one of the most anticipated tracks of the year, the 10 minutes version of an ageless break up anthem, All Too Well. Nonetheless, the older tracks still proceed to age like a fine wine. But we are also fortunate enough to get to listen to the version Taylor Swift wanted us to hear, such as the new pop beat from the track ‘Girl At Home’.Taylor Swift’s matured voice makes the classics such as ‘22’ and ‘I Knew You Were Trouble’ feel better and proves how it deserves the title classic.

While talking about Taylor Swift, it is mandatory to bring up the topic of her heartfelt style of lyric making. It can be seen from one of the From The Vault tracks, such as ‘Nothing New’, a ballad featuring a young singer Phoebe Bridgers, talks about expectations for women in the music industry, where society forced them to be “young” and called out the unfair and sexist critics. It is also expressing the feeling of burn-out coming from the line, “How can a person know everything at 18 but nothing at 22?”. It has become one of the public’s favorites knowing how relatable the lyrics were. But the number one position for the mentioned title is still being held by All Too Well (10 Minutes Version).

Taylor Swift is well known for her way of expressing feelings through her lyrics and the new verses of All Too Well proved the fact right. She proved that it is possible to make a song even sadder than it already is. She made us feel the emotion she felt while writing the lyrics, heck she even made me want to feel a heartbreak from a relationship. Even the way she sings it, the flow, and the way the song goes, it does not even feel like a ten minute song. She made us feel and remember the song, literally, all too well. Quoted from Taylor Swift, who quoted from William Shakespeare, ‘All’s well that ends well”. The journey of getting into a particular place had numerous bumps, it must have numerous bumps in the road, in order for it to end in a beautiful way. The making of Red (Taylor’s Version) might have been a bumpy road, feelings were being hurt and tears were strolled down, but it was all worth it because the result was an embodiment of a masterpiece. It was all worth it, it was all red. (sff)
